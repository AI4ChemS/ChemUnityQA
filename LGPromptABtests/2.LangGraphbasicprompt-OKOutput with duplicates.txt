Mg-MOF-74, a metal-organic framework known for its high CO2 affinity due to open metal sites, can be optimized for better selectivity through functionalization, metal ion substitution, and post-synthetic modifications. This report synthesizes the completed steps and results to provide a comprehensive guide for laboratory implementation.

### Experimental Protocols

#### Protocol 1: Amino Functionalization of Mg-MOF-74

**Scientific Justification:**
Incorporating amino groups can enhance CO2 selectivity by forming strong interactions through hydrogen bonding and acid-base interactions.

**Materials and Equipment:**
- Mg-MOF-74 powder
- 2-aminoterephthalic acid
- Dimethylformamide (DMF)
- Methanol
- Reaction vessel, heating mantle, vacuum oven
- FTIR spectrometer, BET surface area analyzer

**Procedure:**
1. **Preparation of Amino-functionalized Linker Solution:**
   - Dissolve 1 g of 2-aminoterephthalic acid in 50 mL of DMF.
   - Stir at room temperature for 30 minutes.

2. **Functionalization Reaction:**
   - Add 1 g of Mg-MOF-74 to the solution.
   - Heat to 120°C for 24 hours under stirring.

3. **Washing and Activation:**
   - Cool, wash with methanol, and dry in a vacuum oven at 80°C for 12 hours.

4. **Characterization:**
   - Use FTIR for amino group confirmation and BET for surface area analysis.

**Safety and Waste Disposal:**
- Handle DMF and methanol in a fume hood.
- Dispose of waste solvents according to local regulations.

#### Protocol 2: Chromium Substitution in Mg-MOF-74

**Scientific Justification:**
Substituting Mg with Cr can enhance CO2 selectivity by altering the electronic environment and creating stronger interactions with CO2.

**Materials and Equipment:**
- Mg-MOF-74 powder
- Chromium(III) nitrate nonahydrate
- Ethanol
- Reaction vessel, heating mantle, vacuum oven
- XRD equipment, BET surface area analyzer

**Procedure:**
1. **Preparation of Metal Solution:**
   - Dissolve 0.5 g of Chromium(III) nitrate in 50 mL of ethanol.
   - Stir at room temperature for 30 minutes.

2. **Substitution Reaction:**
   - Add 1 g of Mg-MOF-74 to the solution.
   - Heat to 100°C for 48 hours under stirring.

3. **Washing and Activation:**
   - Cool, wash with ethanol, and dry in a vacuum oven at 80°C for 12 hours.

4. **Characterization:**
   - Use XRD for structural confirmation and BET for surface area analysis.

**Safety and Waste Disposal:**
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) when handling chromium salts.
- Dispose of chromium-containing waste according to hazardous waste protocols.
