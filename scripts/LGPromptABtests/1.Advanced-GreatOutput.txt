## Comprehensive Report: Experimental Protocols to Enhance CO2 Selectivity in Mg-MOF-74

### Introduction
The goal of this research was to develop three distinct, lab-ready experimental protocols to increase the CO2 selectivity of Mg-MOF-74 in a simulated flue gas stream (CO2/N2 mixture). Each protocol targets a different modification approach, namely amine functionalization, post-synthetic metal exchange, and incorporation of ionic liquids. These modifications aim to enhance the CO2 adsorption capacity and selectivity of Mg-MOF-74, a metal-organic framework known for its high surface area and open metal sites.

### Protocol 1: Amine Functionalization of Mg-MOF-74

#### Scientific Justification
Amine functionalization introduces basic sites that interact strongly with acidic CO2 molecules, improving the material's performance in gas capture applications.

#### Reagents and Equipment
- **Reagents:**
  - Magnesium nitrate hexahydrate (Mg(NO3)2·6H2O)
  - 2,5-dihydroxyterephthalic acid
  - Dimethylformamide (DMF)
  - Methanol or ethanol
  - Ethylenediamine or other primary amine

- **Equipment:**
  - Solvothermal reaction vessel
  - Vacuum oven
  - Rotary evaporator
  - FTIR spectrometer
  - BET surface area analyzer

#### Step-by-Step Procedure
1. **Synthesis of Mg-MOF-74:**
   - Dissolve 2.0 g of magnesium nitrate hexahydrate and 1.0 g of 2,5-dihydroxyterephthalic acid in 100 mL of DMF.
   - Transfer to a solvothermal reaction vessel and heat at 120°C for 24 hours.
   - Cool to room temperature.

2. **Activation:**
   - Wash crystals with fresh DMF three times.
   - Dry under vacuum at 150°C for 12 hours.

3. **Amine Grafting:**
   - Prepare a 0.1 M solution of ethylenediamine in methanol.
   - Immerse activated Mg-MOF-74 in the solution and stir at room temperature for 24 hours.

4. **Washing and Drying:**
   - Wash with methanol three times.
   - Dry under vacuum at 100°C for 6 hours.

5. **Characterization:**
   - Use FTIR to confirm amine groups.
   - Perform BET analysis for surface area and porosity.

### Protocol 2: Post-Synthetic Metal Exchange with Cu(II)

#### Scientific Justification
Introducing Cu(II) ions can enhance CO2 selectivity due to their strong interaction with CO2 molecules.

#### Reagents and Equipment
- **Reagents:**
  - Mg-MOF-74 (1 g)
  - Copper(II) nitrate trihydrate (Cu(NO3)2·3H2O) (0.5 g)
  - Deionized water (100 mL)

- **Equipment:**
  - Beaker
  - Magnetic stirrer
  - Filtration apparatus
  - Vacuum oven

#### Step-by-Step Procedure
1. **Preparation of Cu(II) Solution:**
   - Dissolve 0.5 g of Cu(NO3)2·3H2O in 100 mL of deionized water.

2. **Immersion of MOF:**
   - Add 1 g of Mg-MOF-74 to the solution and stir at 200 rpm for 48 hours at room temperature.

3. **Filtration and Washing:**
   - Filter the solid and wash with deionized water.

4. **Drying:**
   - Dry in a vacuum oven at 80°C for 24 hours.

5. **Characterization:**
   - Use ICP-MS for metal content.
   - XRD for structural integrity.

#### Design of Experiments (DOE) Table

| Variable          | Range          | Step Size |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| Cu(II) Concentration (M) | 0.01 - 0.1 M   | 0.01 M    |
| Reaction Temperature (°C) | 20 - 40°C      | 5°C       |
| pH Level          | 4.0 - 7.0      | 0.5       |
| Reaction Time (hours) | 12 - 48 hours | 6 hours   |

### Protocol 3: Incorporation of Ionic Liquids

#### Scientific Justification
Ionic liquids can improve CO2 selectivity by providing a medium with high CO2 solubility and selectivity.

#### Reagents and Equipment
- **Reagents:**
  - Mg-MOF-74 (1 g)
  - 1-Butyl-3-methylimidazolium hexafluorophosphate ([BMIM][PF6]) (5 mL)
  - Acetonitrile (50 mL)

- **Equipment:**
  - Round-bottom flask
  - Magnetic stirrer
  - Rotary evaporator
  - Vacuum oven

#### Step-by-Step Procedure
1. **Preparation:**
   - Dissolve 1 g of Mg-MOF-74 in 50 mL of acetonitrile in a round-bottom flask.

2. **Addition of Ionic Liquid:**
   - Add 5 mL of [BMIM][PF6] to the solution.

3. **Stirring:**
   - Stir at 250 rpm for 24 hours at room temperature.

4. **Solvent Removal:**
   - Use a rotary evaporator to remove the solvent.

5. **Drying:**
   - Dry in a vacuum oven at 70°C for 12 hours.

6. **Characterization:**
   - Use PXRD for crystallinity.
   - BET for surface area.

| Variable          | Range          | Step Size |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| [BMIM][PF6] (mL)  | 2-10           | 2         |
| Stirring Speed (rpm) | 100-300      | 50        |
| Drying Temperature (°C) | 50-90     | 10        |
